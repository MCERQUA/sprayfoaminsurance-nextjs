What is a Surety Bond?

A surety bond is not insurance for your business. It is a three-party financial guarantee that ensures you will fulfill your obligations, whether that's following state licensing laws or completing a contracted project.

1

The Principal (You)

The spray foam contractor who purchases the bond to guarantee their work or compliance with state regulations.

2

The Obligee (Client/State)

The entity requiring the bond (like a state licensing board or a project owner) who is protected if you fail to perform.

3

The Surety (Bond Company)

The financial institution that issues the bond, backing your guarantee to the Obligee.

State Licensing Requirements

A Contractor License Bond is not optional in most jurisdictions—it is a legal prerequisite. Without it, your state licensing board will not activate or renew your contractor license, halting your ability to operate legally.

How Much Does a Bond Cost?

You do not pay the full bond amount. You only pay a small annual premium, which is a percentage of the total bond requirement based primarily on your personal credit score.

Typical Premium Range

1-3%

Of the total bond amount per year for contractors with good credit.

Example Scenario: $10,000 State License Bond

If you have Excellent Credit (>700):

$100 / year (approx. 1%)

If you have Average Credit (~650):

Bond vs. General Liability Insurance

Feature Surety Bond General Liability
Who it protects The Client or State (Obligee) Your Business (The Insured)
Purpose Guarantees compliance and performance Covers 3rd party bodily injury/property damage
Claim Responsibility You must repay the Surety for any claims paid Insurance pays; you don't repay (just deductibles)
Premium Structure 1-3% of bond amount (credit-based) Based on payroll/revenue/risk profile

The Annual Renewal Process

Most license bonds are valid for a 1-year, 2-year, or 3-year term. To ensure continuity of your state license, your bond must be renewed before it expires.

We proactively send renewal notices 60-90 days prior to expiration. For many contractors, renewal simply requires paying the invoice—no new credit checks needed unless requesting a limit increase.
