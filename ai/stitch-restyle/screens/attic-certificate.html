The Gold Standard of Documentation

An Attic Insulation Certificate is a formal, legally recognized document that verifies the installation of insulation materials. It serves as permanent proof of the property's thermal envelope upgrade.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this certificate required by law? expand_more
Yes, the International Residential Code (IRC) requires an insulation certificate to be posted in a conspicuous location (often near the electrical panel) following new installation or significant upgrades.
How long should homeowners keep the certificate? expand_more
Homeowners should retain the certificate permanently with their home's vital records. It is often required when selling the property to prove the quality and specs of the thermal envelope.
Can I use my own custom form? expand_more
While custom forms are allowed if they meet all IRC requirements, using a standardized, industry-recognized form reduces pushback from inspectors and makes the rebate process smoother for clients.
Does SFI charge per certificate generated? expand_more
No. Unlimited access to the Digital Issuance Tool is included as a complimentary risk-management feature for all active SFI policyholders.
How do I calculate the final R-value for SPF? expand_more
The final R-value is calculated by multiplying the manufacturer's stated aged R-value per inch by the average measured depth of the installed foam across the application area.
What if a client loses their printed copy? expand_more
If generated through the SFI portal, you can easily search for the past job by address or date and instantly email them a replacement PDF copy.
